Taylo Swift


Taylor Alison Swift (born December 13, 1989) is an American singer and songwriter . She is known for her narrative style, artistic versatility, and cultural influence . Swift is considered a prominent figure in popular music and a center of public attention . Swift began pursuing a professional music career at the age of 14. She signed with Big Machine Records in 2005, becoming a country artist and has been referred to as the "princess of country music" ever since. At Big Machine Records, Swift released six studio albums, with the first four being sent to country radio, starting with her debut album Taylor Swift (2006). She followed that with Fearless (2008), which explored pop and country, and the singles " Love Story " and " You Belong with Me " which helped her gain mainstream recognition. Swift then released her third studio album, Speak Now (2010), which was heavily influenced by rock, and her fourth studio album Red (2012), which added electronic elements , spawning her first Billboard Hot 100 number-one single , " We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together ". In 2014, Swift shed her "country princess" image and released her fifth studio album, 1989 (2014), which drew inspiration from 1980s synth-pop and featured the chart-topping singles " Shake It Off ", " Blank Space ", and " Bad Blood ". The media scrutiny inspired her sixth studio album Reputation (2017), which was influenced by hip hop , and featured the number-one single " Look What You Made Me Do ".
